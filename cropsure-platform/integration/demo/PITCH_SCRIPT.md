## [0:45 — 1:30] THE PROBLEM

"The existing insurance model requires a human to physically visit each farm,
assess the damage, write a report, and submit a claim.

On a 2-acre farm in rural Nakuru, that process costs more than the claim is worth.
So insurance companies don't serve smallholder farmers.
And farmers are left completely exposed.

Two companies have tried to solve this — ACRE Africa and Pula.
They use satellite data and regional weather indices to trigger automatic payouts.
And they proved the market exists.

But they have one unsolved problem: basis risk.
A farmer's crops can fail completely while the regional index says everything is fine.
She gets no payout. She loses trust. She never renews.

That is the problem we solve."

## ANTICIPATED JUDGE QUESTIONS

Q: ACRE Africa already does this.
A: ACRE uses regional weather indices. We monitor the exact farm polygon
at 10-metre resolution. That is the difference between insuring a county
and insuring your farm.

Q: What about cloud cover?
A: We fuse Sentinel-2 optical data with Sentinel-1 SAR radar.
SAR penetrates clouds and additionally detects flooding.
This dual-sensor approach is a direct technical advance over all current products.

Q: How do you calibrate the model?
A: Historical NDVI data per crop type per region going back to 2015,
correlated with CHIRPS rainfall data and KALRO yield records.
We plan to partner with KALRO for ongoing ground truth validation.

Q: Are you a licensed insurer?
A: We are the technology layer. We follow ACRE's distribution model —
partnering with licensed insurers rather than becoming one directly.
This is faster to market and legally cleaner.

Q: What is your biggest risk?
A: Farmer adoption. We mitigate this through agent-assisted enrollment,
Swahili language support, USSD access for feature phones,
and transparent payouts that build trust from the first claim.
